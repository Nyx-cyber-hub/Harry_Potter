About the Magic

In the world of Harry Potter, magic is the hidden force that shapes the lives of witches and wizards. Unlike the non-magical people called Muggles, wizards are born with the ability to harness magical energy and cast spells using a wand.

Magic is taught at the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where students learn subjects such as:

  • Charms ‚Äì spells that add special properties to objects or creatures.
  • Transfiguration ‚Äì the art of changing the form or appearance of something.
  • Potions ‚Äì magical mixtures with powerful effects.
  • Defense Against the Dark Arts ‚Äì protection against curses and dark creatures.
  • Herbology ‚Äì study of magical plants and their uses.

Spells range from simple ones like Lumos (to produce light) to powerful curses like the Avada Kedavra (the Killing Curse). Some magic is used for everyday tasks, while others are meant for battle or healing.

Magic in Harry Potter is not only about power ‚Äî it also represents choices, responsibility, and morality. The series shows that true strength comes not from the spells themselves, but from how they are used.
